Project Overview

The Duval Audubon Society in Northeast Florida connects people with nature, with the goal of conserving and restoring ecosystems and focusing on birds and other wildlife. For my final project, my group and I were given the opportunity to redesign 3 pages of a website to improve the overall information architecture and design.

My Role

As project manager, I was tasked with keeping control over the entire project. This consisted of defining the scope of the project, performing daily stand up meetings with the team, and making principal decisions to have an appealing design in the 3 weeks we had.

My team assigned me to be the lead coder to successfully create our fully developed website.

Design Process

My team followed a 4 step Design Thinking process. The stages of the process are as follows:

Discover

User Interviews, User Insight, Problem Statement, SWOT Analysis, Competitor Analysis, Affinity Mapping

Define

User Persona, Competitor Analysis, Style Guide, User Flow, User Testing, Hi-Fi Prototyping

Develop

HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap, GitHub, Guerilla Testing Plan, Guerilla Testing, Final Coded Website

Deliver

Our team delivered the completed project and website to the class and instructors. We received an A+.

Design Toolkit

1. Discover

The Problem

Potential Duval Audubon Society members struggle to navigate the current website and are unimpressed with its design. How might we improve information architecture and design elements to attract more members?

The Solution

Create a welcoming and colorful redesign that is effortless to navigate and attract new members and supporters. Users can get involved from the homepage, donate from the secondary navigation bar, and explore recent sightings.

Research

To discover what people perceived as the challenges of navigating the Duval Audubon website, my team set out to conduct primary research by interviewing candidates . Our objective was to evaulate the main pain points experienced by potential Audubon members and how we might ease these frustrations. A team member created a Google Survey to ask further questions to potential candidates to participate in interviews.

Old Design - Home, Get Involved, Conservation Pages

Home Page Problems

  • The older homepage had no structure, with a text heavy body copy and unprofessional images, creating readability issues for users.
  • The green background color and overall color palette is weak, leading to accessibility concerns.
  • The introduction is clear, yet can be improved to encourage the user to keep reading.

Get Involved Page Problems

  • The page is boring and contains several grammatical errors and dead links.
  • It is difficult to understand who each director is with the current layout.
  • There is not a way to get involved from the dropdown menu and Get Involved Page, which is misleading to the user.

Conservation Page Problems

  • The amount of text and bolded text makes it impossible to follow along, particularly in a z-pattern.
  • This section is cluttered as there is no breathing space available for the content and images.
  • The images add unnecessary space, as do the amount of exit points which increases the decision time taken by the user.

New Design

Solutions

  • The redesigned pages includes a strong and bold hero section to properly introduce Duval.
  • The pages are assigned a bright color from the style guide, as shown in the secondary navigation, headers, and footer.
  • We used an image rather a block of text for the hero, as it takes users as little as 50 milliseconds to create an opinion about a website.

The redesigned pages have several different sections and are of good scroll length. There are no two consecutive sections of the layout, avoiding a monotonous webpage for the user. This influnces the user to scroll for a longer duration.

The style guide is implemented in a unique way to avoid overwhelming the user with too many bright colors that may create accessibility issues. There is adequate amount of whitespace given to each section with a pop of color.

Important aspects of the society are shown upfront, reinforcing the purpose of the webpage.

The amount of body copy was reduced hence eliminating readability issues.

There is more structure in layout and content, along with images to personalize the webpage.
